Harappan Scripts

  • Indus Script or Harrapan Script which was used by people of Harrapa, is short strings of symbols associated with Indus Valley Civilisation.

  • Inspite of many attempts at decipherments claims, it is as yet undeciphered.

About The Script...

  • Unlikely other scripts, the direction of writing of this script is right to left

  • Harrapa and mohenjodaro exhibit urban planning that rival modern standards, including grid-like streets, water management and the oldest toilets.

  • Since 2004, there's even been a standing color $ 10,000 prize for anyone who discovers an Indus text over 50 characters

  • It was once supposed that the script is similar to the archetype of the Brahmi script used by Ashoka
